Market Sim — Ledoit-Wolf Shrinkage + EWMA

12 simulated stocks move every second with bimodal returns. Hidden correlations link the stocks, but the front-end never sees them directly. A Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage estimator on top of an Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA) covariance recovers the correlation structure from observed returns alone. This is what real quantitative finance desks use — it's faster and more accurate than MCMC for this problem because:

  • EWMA adapts to regime changes (crash/boost) within a few ticks instead of needing a full window to flush old data
  • Shrinkage regularises the noisy sample covariance toward a stable target (scaled identity), dramatically cutting estimation error when the number of observations isn't much larger than the number of stocks
  • The shrinkage intensity is computed analytically — no sampling, no convergence issues, O(p²·T) per tick
