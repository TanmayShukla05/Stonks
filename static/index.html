Market Sim with MCMC Correlation Estimation

12 simulated stocks move every second. Each stock's return is drawn from a bimodal distribution (two humps, not one) so it tends to jump clearly up or down rather than drift quietly. Stocks are secretly correlated to each other by the server, and those correlations are never sent to this page directly — instead, a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithm watches the realized price history and estimates the correlations itself using Metropolis-Hastings sampling over a rolling 50-tick window. That estimate then feeds a simple factor model: each stock's predicted next move is a correlation-weighted average of what its (estimated) correlated peers just did, which is turned into the BUY/SELL confidence shown under each chart. The "Show guessed correlations" button reveals how far off the model's guesses are from the true hidden correlations, as an error table (guessed − true) plus a single mean absolute error number.

Disrupt forces a stock's next move to be drawn from a strongly negative bimodal (peaks around -4× to -6× base volatility). Enhance does the same on the upside. Because of the hidden correlations, this can visibly move other stocks too. The dot next to each price is green if the price is higher than it was 30 ticks ago, red if lower, and black if unchanged or not enough history yet.
