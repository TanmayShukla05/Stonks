Market Sim

12 simulated stocks move every second. Each stock's return is drawn from a bimodal distribution (two humps, not one) so it tends to jump clearly up or down rather than drift quietly. Stocks are secretly correlated to each other by the server, and those correlations are never sent to this page directly — instead, a small model below watches the realized price history and estimates the correlations itself (using Pearson correlation over a rolling 50-tick window). That estimate then feeds a simple factor model: each stock's predicted next move is a correlation-weighted average of what its (estimated) correlated peers just did, which is turned into the BUY/SELL confidence shown under each chart.

Disrupt forces a stock's next move to be drawn from a strongly negative bimodal (peaks around -4× to -6× base volatility). Enhance does the same on the upside. Because of the hidden correlations, this can visibly move other stocks too.
